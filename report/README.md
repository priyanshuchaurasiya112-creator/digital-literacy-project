
Task 1 Write-up:

I created a digital literacy awareness infographic using Canva, which is an easy-to-use online design tool. The purpose of this infographic is to help students understand the importance of digital literacy in today’s academic and professional environment. My design covers key topics such as the meaning of digital literacy, useful digital tools for students, safe internet practices, maintaining a professional online presence, and proper email etiquette.

While designing the infographic, I focused on making the content visually appealing and easy to understand by using icons, headings, and concise points. I ensured that the information was clear and relevant for students who are just beginning to explore digital platforms.

One challenge I faced was selecting the right layout and balancing the amount of content without overcrowding the design. However, Canva’s templates helped me organize the information effectively. Overall, this task helped me understand how to present important information in a simple and engaging visual format.

Task 4 Write-up (100–150 words)

In this task, I learned the importance of professional communication through email writing and social media etiquette. I drafted two formal emails—one requesting an assignment extension from a professor and another expressing interest in a summer internship. This helped me understand how to structure emails properly using a clear subject line, polite tone, and professional sign-off.

A situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an informal or unclear email to a professor, which may lead to misunderstanding or being ignored. For example, using casual language or not mentioning the purpose clearly can create confusion.

This could be improved by using proper greetings, clearly stating the purpose, and maintaining a respectful tone. Overall, this task helped me realize the importance of clear and professional communication in academic and professional environments.

Task 5 Write-up (100–150 words)

While working on the cybercrime awareness task, I chose to study phishing, which is a common type of online fraud. I was surprised to learn how easily attackers can trick people into sharing sensitive information by creating fake emails or websites that look genuine. It made me realize that even small mistakes, like clicking on unknown links, can lead to serious consequences such as financial loss or identity theft.

This task helped me become more aware of online safety practices, especially while using emails and online payment systems. One habit I will change is being more cautious while opening links or messages from unknown sources and always verifying the authenticity before taking any action.

Overall, this task increased my awareness of cyber threats and encouraged me to adopt safer digital practices in my daily life.

These tools will be very beneficial for my academic journey, as HackerRank will help me improve my programming skills, and Google Forms will assist in conducting surveys, quizzes, and collaborative academic activities.
